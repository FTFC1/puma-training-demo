‚úÖ The Method: 5 Warm Openings

These are the lines that actually work. They're open-ended, conversational, and impossible to shut down with "no."

1 The Occasion Question
"What brings you in today?"
Open-ended. Invites them to explain their need. Feels conversational, not salesy.
Example Dialogue
You: "Hey! What brings you in today?"
Customer: "Just looking for running shoes."
You: "Nice ‚Äî what kind of running do you do?"
2 The Choice Frame
"Are you looking for something specific, or just seeing what's new?"
Gives them two options ‚Äî both involve engagement. There's no "no" option.
Example Dialogue
You: "Looking for something specific, or just seeing what's new?"
Customer: "Just seeing what you have."
You: "Cool ‚Äî we just got new arrivals this week. What style do you usually go for?"
3 The Product Hook
"That [item] is one of our most popular ‚Äî [feature] is what people love. Would you like to try?"
You're giving useful info + inviting action. Not asking if they need help.
Example Dialogue
You: "That Suede Classic is one of our best sellers ‚Äî super versatile, goes with everything. What size are you?"
Customer: "10."
You: "Let me grab you a 10 to try."
4 The Self/Gift Question
"Are you shopping for yourself or looking for a gift?"
Everyone is doing one or the other. Forces them to engage.
Example Dialogue
You: "Are you shopping for yourself or a gift?"
Customer: "Gift for my brother."
You: "Nice! What's the occasion ‚Äî birthday?"
5 The Compliment Bridge
"I love those [shoes/jacket] ‚Äî are you looking for something similar or something different?"
Compliments build instant rapport. Then you're giving them a choice, not yes/no.
Example Dialogue
You: "Those RS-Xs are clean ‚Äî looking for more sneakers or something different today?"
Customer: "More sneakers, yeah."
You: "What's your style ‚Äî classic or the new stuff?"
