‚úÖ The Method: 70/30 Rule

The Rule
70%
Customer Talks
30%
You Talk
If you're doing most of the talking, you're doing it wrong.
Your job during browsing is NOT to show products. It's to understand what they need ‚Äî then show the RIGHT product.
‚úÖ Do: Ask questions ‚Üí Listen ‚Üí Follow up ‚Üí THEN recommend
‚ùå Don't: Show everything ‚Üí Talk about features ‚Üí Hope something sticks

üéØ The 4 Questions Framework

Before you show a single product, understand these four things:

1 The Occasion
"What are you buying for?"
Not "I need shoes" ‚Äî dig deeper. "I'm running my first marathon." "I need something for work that's comfortable." "My old ones fell apart."
Follow-ups
  • "Where will you be wearing this?"
  • "What activities do you need this for?"
  • "What's coming up that made you come in today?"
2 The Problem
"What's not working with what you have now?"
Every customer is here because something isn't working. Find out what. This tells you which features actually matter.
Follow-ups
  • "What do you wish your current one did better?"
  • "Have you tried anything before that didn't work?"
  • "What's frustrating you about your current setup?"
3 The Priority
"What matters most ‚Äî comfort, durability, or style?"
Everyone weighs these differently. Some don't care what it looks like if it's comfortable. Others need it to look good above everything.
Follow-ups
  • "Do you have a budget in mind?"
  • "Is brand important to you?"
  • "Looking for something to last years, or just this season?"
4 The Decision Context
"Are you hoping to leave with something today?"
Buying today or researching? For themselves or a gift? Alone or need to consult someone? This tells you how to close.
Follow-ups
  • "Is this for you or someone else?"
  • "Is there anyone else involved in the decision?"
  • "How soon do you need this?"
